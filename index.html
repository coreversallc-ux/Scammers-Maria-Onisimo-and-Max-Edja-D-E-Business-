Executive Summary

This formal complaint is submitted to the Small Business Administration (SBA) regarding fraudulent activity involving a Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) loan under the business entity Core Versatility LLC.

I am writing to report that Maria Onesimo, my former business partner and co-owner of Core Versatility LLC, fraudulently applied for and received a PPP loan in April 2020 without my knowledge or consent. This loan, totaling over $20,000, was obtained under false pretenses and in direct violation of federal law.

Key Facts

  • Business Entity: Core Versatility LLC (Colorado-based, 50-50 ownership)
  • Co-Owners: [Complainant Name] (50%) and Maria Onesimo (50%)
  • Loan Amount: Over $20,000 in federal PPP funds
  • Date of Fraudulent Application: April 2020
  • Lending Institution: Colorado Lending Source Ltd. (now managed by B:Side)
  • Accountant Involved: Max Edja / D&E Business

Nature of the Fraud

Maria Onesimo, without my knowledge or authorization, submitted a PPP loan application on behalf of Core Versatility LLC. As a 50% co-owner of the company, I was never consulted, never signed any loan documents, and never received any portion of the funds. The loan was processed and disbursed entirely without my involvement, despite the fact that my ownership stake should have required my consent and signature.

This constitutes clear and unambiguous fraud under federal law, as the PPP program requires all business owners to be aware of and consent to loan applications. Maria Onesimo's actions represent both fraud against me as a co-owner and fraud against the federal government.

Background & Context

Core Versatility LLC was established in May 2019 as a 50-50 partnership between myself and Maria Onesimo. The business was officially registered with the Colorado Secretary of State, with both parties listed as equal co-owners.

In April 2020, during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, Maria Onesimo, in collaboration with accountant Max Edja (D&E Business), applied for and received a PPP loan totaling over $20,000 through Colorado Lending Source Ltd. (now managed by B:Side). This loan application was submitted entirely without my knowledge or consent.

I first became aware of this fraudulent loan in 2024 when I received correspondence regarding the loan status and repayment obligations. Upon investigation, I discovered that Maria Onesimo had forged my signature on loan documents, falsely represented herself as having authorization to act on behalf of the company, and diverted all loan proceeds for her own personal use.

Timeline of Events

  • May 27, 2019: Core Versatility LLC officially formed with 50-50 ownership structure
  • April 2020: PPP loan fraudulently applied for and received by Maria Onesimo
  • 2020-2024: Loan proceeds diverted; complainant kept unaware of loan existence
  • 2024: Complainant discovers fraudulent loan and initiates complaint process
  • February 29, 2024: Separate civil lawsuit filed against Maria Onesimo for fraud (San Diego Superior Court)

Key Evidence

The following evidence substantiates the allegations of fraud and demonstrates the unauthorized nature of the PPP loan application:

1. Loan Application Documents

The PPP loan application submitted to Colorado Lending Source Ltd. contains forged signatures and false representations. The application lists Maria Onesimo as the sole authorized representative of Core Versatility LLC, despite the 50-50 ownership structure requiring consent from both co-owners.

2. Email Correspondence

Email communications between Maria Onesimo and the lending institution clearly demonstrate her sole involvement in the loan process, with no evidence of my participation or knowledge. These emails show deliberate efforts to conceal the loan application from me.

3. Disbursement Records

Bank records and disbursement authorizations show that all loan proceeds were directed to accounts controlled exclusively by Maria Onesimo, with no distribution to me as a 50% co-owner. This establishes both the fraudulent intent and the resulting financial harm.

4. Colorado Secretary of State Records

Official state records confirm the 50-50 ownership structure of Core Versatility LLC, establishing my legal right to be consulted on all major financial decisions, including loan applications.

5. Articles of Organization

The founding documents of Core Versatility LLC, filed under oath with the Colorado Secretary of State, explicitly list both Maria Onesimo and myself as equal co-owners, each holding a 50% ownership stake.

Requested Actions

I respectfully request that the Small Business Administration (SBA) and relevant federal authorities take the following actions:

  • Investigate this matter as PPP loan fraud: Conduct a thorough investigation into the fraudulent PPP loan application submitted by Maria Onesimo under Core Versatility LLC.
  • Determine the legality of the loan application: Review all loan documentation, signatures, and authorization procedures to establish the fraudulent nature of the application.
  • Hold all responsible parties fully accountable: Take appropriate legal action against Maria Onesimo, Max Edja / D&E Business, and any other individuals or entities involved in this fraudulent scheme.
  • Recover fraudulently obtained funds: Pursue the recovery of all PPP loan proceeds that were obtained through fraudulent means.
  • Protect my interests as a co-owner: Ensure that I am not held liable for loan obligations that I never authorized or benefited from.
  • Refer for criminal prosecution: If warranted by the investigation, refer this matter to the Department of Justice for criminal prosecution under applicable federal fraud statutes.
